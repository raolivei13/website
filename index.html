Richard Oliveira

Hello! My name is Richard, and I am a Junior Data Scientist working on LLMs for a startup.

I received my MSc. in Electrical Engineering, Signal and Image Processing and a BSc. in Electrical Engineering, Machine Learning and Controls from the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at the University of California, San Diego, La Jolla, USA.

My background can be broadly defined as that of a Mathematical Engineer. My research interests lie at the intersection of Functional Analysis, Machine Learning, and Optimization, with a primary focus on the mathematics of Data Science. My work centers on developing foundational understanding of Deep Neural Networks (DNNs), exploring critical questions such as the effect of regularization, the nature of functions learned by Neural Networks (NNs), and their ability to overcome dimensional complexity. Key research interests include investigating the mathematical foundations of Deep Learning and examining the connections between Inverse Problems and NN function learning, with potential applications in Control Theory and Robotics.

Research keywords of particular interest include:
  • Inverse Problems
  • Geometry of Banach Spaces
  • Bayesian and Discrete Optimization
  • Non-Parametric Statistics
  • Model Predictive Control (MPC)
